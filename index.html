What is the debt to capital ratio?
The debt to capital ratio to capital ratio is a measurement of the firm's financial laverage and it is calculated by taking the firm's short and long term liabilities and dividing it by the total capital which is the debt plus shareholder's equity.
How to calculate debt to capital ratio?
To calculate debt to capital ratio you should use the following formula:
debt to capital ratio = debt / (debt + shareholder's equity)
Example: company x has $6,300,000 in total debt and id has $2,000,000 worth of shareholder's equity, calculate the debt to capital ratio?
Using the debt to capital ratio the answer will be:
debt to capital ratio = debt / (debt + shareholder's equity)
debt to capital ratio = $6,300,000 / ($6,300,000 + $2,000,000)
debt to capital ratio = 75.9%
Why use debt to capital ratio?
Analysts and investors use the debt to capital ratio because it gives them a better adia of the company's financial structure and if the company is a good investment or not. High debt to capital ratio means that the company is risky for inbesments.
